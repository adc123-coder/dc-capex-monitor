Monthly Commentary — April 2026
1
Composite remains negative, driven entirely by the private round velocity collapse: Both public credit indicators are now positive contributors vs. the Apr '24 baseline. The composite staying below zero is a private-market story, not a public credit story. The distinction matters analytically: the broad credit channel for AI is healthy; the early-stage venture channel is stressed.
2
CoreWeave CDS tightened sharply from the Dec '25 peak of 773bps to ~460bps: Meta ($21B expansion), Anthropic (multi-year cloud deal), Jane Street ($6B), and a $2.75B HY note offering priced at 102 have materially improved credit sentiment around the neocloud tier. At 20% weight this is a modest positive pull. Still elevated vs. the pre-2025 proxy baseline — the credit market is not declaring CoreWeave investment-grade — but the direction is unambiguously positive.
3
ICE BofA HY/IG differential at ~204bps — tighter than the Apr '24 start of ~240bps: Broad HY OAS ~284bps, IG ~80bps as of April 2026 — near 25-year tights for IG. The AI credit market has been confirmed as a "juggernaut" by Bloomberg (Apr 11), with AI-linked high-grade debt increasingly appealing to institutional buyers. At 40% weight this is the composite's largest positive offset to the round-velocity drag.
4
Private round velocity at 27 — the sole negative sub-component and the composite's anchor: Down from 48 in Apr '24 and 50% below the Jun '25 peak. Down-round share at 23%. This component (40% weight) is the reason the composite is negative even as public credit tightens. The divergence between public credit (healthy) and private venture (stressed) suggests the stress is concentrated in pre-revenue or thin-backlog AI companies that cannot access the public debt markets that CoreWeave and hyperscalers can.
5
Key watch condition: does private round velocity recover? Historically it lags public credit improvement by one full quarter. If the CoreWeave-led credit rehabilitation broadens into improved LP sentiment and re-opened Series B/C pipelines by Jun–Jul '26, the composite could recover to near-zero. The down-round share stabilising below 20% would be the first confirmation signal.
