Monthly Commentary — May 2026
1
Index has moved from −2.3 in April to an improved reading in May — first MoM gain since Aug '25: All three sub-components contributed positively: CDS tightened ~10bps, HY/IG held near tights, and round velocity bounced from 27 to 29. The composite crossed a meaningful threshold — breaking a nine-month streak of either deterioration or flat readings. It remains comfortably negative, but the direction has changed. This is the first month the data supports a stabilisation narrative rather than a stress narrative.
2
CDS tightening is the sharpest positive MoM move — and it has legs: The $3.1B CoreWeave leveraged loan arranged by Morgan Stanley/MUFG attracted $15–19B in orders (5–6× oversubscribed) and priced with margins tightening 50bps to SOFR+450. That is the single clearest market signal that institutional credit appetite for tier-1 neocloud paper has recovered from the Dec peak. Q1 earnings were mixed (rev +112% beat; EPS missed by 54%; backlog +50% to $99.4B) — but the loan market looked through the P&L miss and focused on backlog. CDS at ~450bps is ~320bps inside the Dec '25 peak.
3
HY/IG differential at ~205bps — stable for four consecutive months and the composite's anchor to positive territory: Broad HY near 25-year tights, IG at ~80bps. This component has been absorbing the private-market stress for the entire period and has not widened. At 40% weight it is preventing the composite from reflecting the full severity implied by round velocity alone. The AI credit "juggernaut" characterisation from Bloomberg in April has held through May — AI-linked issuance remains bid.
4
Round velocity at 29 — a bounce, but the structural story is unchanged: The apparent Q1 2026 VC boom ($300B+ globally) is a three-deal story: OpenAI $122B, xAI $20B, Anthropic $30.6B. Strip those out and Series B activity dipped QoQ, seed deal counts fell 30–40%, and capital concentration intensified. The 29 current reading is still 39% below the Apr '24 baseline of 48 and 46% below the Jun '25 peak. A recovery in this component — which carries 40% weight — is what would move the composite out of negative territory. It has not started yet.
5
The recovery path is CDS-led with round velocity as the gating condition: If Q2 earnings (Aug 18) confirm backlog-to-revenue conversion — CoreWeave guided $12–13B FY revenue with an $18–19B exit run rate — CDS should continue toward the 350–380bps range, adding further composite improvement. But the composite cannot reach neutral until round velocity stabilises above ~38 deals/mo. The gap between a public-credit recovery (already happening) and a private-market recovery (not yet) defines the remaining downside in the index.
