Key Takeaways — May 2026
▸ Backlog and B2B accelerating, not plateauing: Siemens Energy hit a new all-time high of €154B in orders with B2B at 1.72 (Q2 FY2026, May 12). Eaton total Electrical backlog +48% YoY to ~$22.8B with DC orders up 240% in Q1 2026 — Siemens SI backlog reached a record €22B with B2B 1.27 (Q2 FY2026, May 21). No OEM is guiding to order deceleration.
▸ Large transformer lead times confirmed at 128–144 weeks and holding: Wood Mackenzie Q2 2025 survey (most recent systematic data): ≥100MVA power transformers avg 128 weeks, GSUs 144 weeks, some units above 200 weeks. MV switchgear ~44 weeks. New capacity (Eaton SC, Siemens NC, Hitachi VA) won't reach meaningful output before mid-2028. Prices 4–6× their 2022 level.
▸ Hyperscalers locking up factory slots directly: GEV booked $2.4B in DC equipment in Q1 alone. Eaton's DC backlog equivalent to 12 years of output at 2025 build rates (228 GW). Siemens Energy U.S. orders more than doubled QoQ, largely driven by data centers. The residual market for utilities and smaller developers is structurally shrinking. B2B is one of the earliest signals of the DC capex cycle.
