Why DDR5 Leads Capex
Memory is one of the first items in the procurement chain. Spot prices move before the contract market, and both move before capex hits filings. By the time NVIDIA reports a beat, the memory for those servers was ordered two quarters earlier. DDR5 is in structural short supply — Samsung/SK hynix/Micron allocation is exhausted at the contract level, which means spot pricing becomes a direct read on hyperscalers' willingness to secure capacity well ahead of deployment. The grey-market spread confirms active panic-buying from second-tier DC operators — neoclouds and sovereigns paying premiums to get allocation at any cost. When that premium compresses, the urgency to pre-order is fading — typically 2 quarters before reported capex peaks.
