Key Takeaways — April 2026
▸ B2B ratios at historic extremes: GE Vernova's Electrification segment posted a 2.5x B2B in Q1 2026 — orders more than doubled while revenues grew 61%. Siemens Energy Grid Technologies reported 1.82x. Blended 6-OEM B2B now ~1.55, the highest in this dataset. All six OEMs guided to continued positive B2B for the remainder of 2026.
▸ Large transformer lead times confirmed at 128–144 weeks: Wood Mackenzie Q2 2025 survey (most recent) puts ≥100MVA power transformers at 128 weeks avg, GSUs at 144 weeks. Some units now quoted above 200 weeks. MV switchgear eased marginally to ~44 weeks but remains structurally elevated. No relief expected before mid-2028 as new capacity (Eaton SC, Siemens NC, Hitachi VA) won't reach scale until then.
▸ Hyperscalers now dominating the factory slot market: GEV booked $2.4B in DC equipment in Q1 alone — more than all of 2025 combined. Siemens SI recorded a record €1.8B in data center orders. Supply is bifurcating: hyperscalers secure capacity via long-term take-or-pay agreements; utilities and smaller developers are competing for the residual. Prices are 4–6× their 2022 levels (T&D World, March 2026).

Commentary — April 2026
1
GE Vernova Q1 2026 — the standout print: Electrification orders +86% organically, B2B ~2.5x, equipment backlog $38.6B (+75% YoY, incl. $5B from Prolec GE acquisition). GEV booked $2.4B in DC equipment in Q1 alone — more than all of 2025 combined. Total RPO now $163B; $200B target pulled forward to 2027 from 2028.
2
Siemens Energy & Siemens SI both at record backlogs: Siemens Energy Grid Technologies B2B at 1.82 (Q1 FY2026), total backlog €146B. Siemens Smart Infrastructure posted €7.2B in orders (quarterly record, +22%), B2B 1.30, backlog €20.2B all-time high — driven by €1.8B in data center orders, half from large single-site contracts.
3
Eaton & ABB confirm broad-based tightening: Eaton total backlog ~$19.6B (+29% YoY), DC orders up ~200%, quarterly B2B above 1.2x. ABB backlog $27.5B with Electrification comparable revenue +15% in Q1 2026; full-year guidance raised to high-single-digit to low-double-digit growth.
4
Lead times confirmed at 128–144 weeks for large transformers: Wood Mackenzie Q2 2025 survey: ≥100MVA avg 128wk, GSUs 144wk. Some units exceeding 200wk. MV switchgear eased to ~44wk. Transformer prices now 4–6× 2022 levels (T&D World, March 2026). New capacity (Eaton SC, Siemens NC, Hitachi VA) won't reach meaningful scale before mid-2028.
5
Market bifurcating between hyperscalers and everyone else: Hyperscalers (Google, Microsoft, AWS) now account for 48% of global DC capacity and are securing transformer slots via long-term take-or-pay agreements directly with GEV, Siemens Energy, Hitachi. Utilities, co-ops, and smaller developers are competing for residual slots — a structural tiering of access that will widen as B2B ratios stay above 1.5.
