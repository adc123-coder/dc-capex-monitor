Monthly Commentary — April 2026
1
Land acquisition remains the composite's anchor — Aligned Data Centers' $2.58B raise confirms hyperscaler conviction: 5,100 acres closed or under LOI in April (+6% MoM), with major activity in Dallas, Phoenix, and Northern Virginia. Aligned's capital raise for US expansion, NTT's 115 MW commitment across Gainesville/Chicago/Sacramento, and Penzance's $4B West Virginia campus signal that long-horizon demand has not wavered. Land is still the most speculative signal — LOI ≠ committed spend — but the volume and quality of transactions in April is unambiguously positive.
2
Construction starts falling sharply — cancellations quadrupled YoY (Baird: 25 in 2025 vs. 6 in 2024) and the trend is accelerating in April: 33 projects breaking ground MTD, down 13% from March, as developers stall on power access and navigate moratorium risk. Projects near regulatory flashpoints — Virginia battlefield corridor, Denver, Georgia Beltline zone — are being shelved or rerouted. Starts are now the weakest signal in the composite and will remain under pressure through Q3 2026 as the weaker Q4 2025 approval cohort works through the pipeline.
3
Approval rate at 57% — new cycle-low — driven by moratorium wave and utility grid constraints simultaneously: At least 12 states have active moratorium bills in session, 63+ local moratorium actions adopted or introduced, and the active block count in our 14-market panel rose to 11 in April (Denver moratorium added). The approval rate is the most concrete indicator; at 57%, roughly 43% of filed capacity is currently being delayed or denied. Without resolution of the Dominion Energy hold or the PJM freeze, expect the rate to remain in the 54–60% range through Q3 2026.
4
Regulatory friction is at a structural inflection — moratorium wave is broadening beyond NIMBYism to bipartisan policy action: Maine's legislature passed the first statewide AI data center moratorium in US history (Apr 22), though the governor vetoed it yesterday (Apr 26) to protect a single rural project. The veto provides temporary relief but signals the political pressure is now sufficient to pass moratoriums at the state legislature level. Two forces are now partially counteracting: Trump's December 2025 executive order pushing federal fast-track permitting, and state/local moratorium legislation. The net effect is likely to be market bifurcation — federally-supported large-campus projects may accelerate while small/mid-scale projects in regulated states face increasing friction.
5
Geographic overflow continues but Denver's moratorium is a new signal for second-tier markets: Phoenix, Dallas, and Columbus continue absorbing displaced Northern Virginia volume. However, Denver — previously a clean overflow destination — has now passed a moratorium, reducing the number of reliable fast-approval markets. Iowa (91% approval), Kansas City (83%), and San Antonio (88%) remain the cleanest pipelines. The universe of low-friction markets is narrowing as the moratorium wave spreads.
Regulatory Blocks & Moratoria — Active Apr 27, 2026
Denver, CO — City Data Center Moratorium
⚑ NEW · Apr '26
Denver announced a moratorium on new data center approvals to reassess regulations, balancing tech growth with environmental and community impact. Timing uncertain but expected through Q3 2026. First clean overflow market in our panel to impose a formal pause.
Maine — Statewide Moratorium Vetoed
VETOED · Apr 26
Governor Mills vetoed LD 307 — the first statewide AI data center moratorium to pass a US legislature — citing impact on a rural project in Jay, Franklin County. Veto provides near-term relief but the legislature passed it 54:9; a veto override is possible. Maine not in our 14-market panel but sets a national legislative precedent.
N. Virginia — Dominion Energy Interconnection Hold
Ongoing · Since Mar 14
Hold on all new large-load interconnection requests (>10 MW) in Loudoun & Prince William counties pending capacity study. ~18 permits / ~2,400 MW directly affected. Estimated resolution: Q3 2026.
N. Virginia — Loudoun County Moratorium
Ongoing · Since Sep '25
Board of Supervisors moratorium extended through Jun 2026 — third extension. No new conditional-use permits within 500ft of residential zones. Virginia HB 1515 (grid-interconnection conditions) adds state-level overlay.
New Jersey — PJM Interconnection Freeze
Ongoing · Since Mar 3
90-day administrative freeze on new large-load interconnection applications in NJ/NYC load zone under FERC Order 2023 reform. Expires early June 2026 unless extended.
Phoenix — ADWR Water Adequacy Review
Watching · Since Feb 28
2 large-campus permits (>500 acres) under enhanced water adequacy review, ARS §45-576. 60–90 day delay per permit. Not yet a formal moratorium.
Chicago — ComEd Transmission Upgrade Delay
Ongoing · Since Nov '25
Manteno–Chicago North 345kV upgrade delayed to Q4 2026. ~280 MW on hold pending capacity confirmation.
