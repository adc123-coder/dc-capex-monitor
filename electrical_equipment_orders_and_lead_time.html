Key Takeaways — March 2026
▸ Broadest constraint in the tracking window: All six OEMs are reporting simultaneous order intake > shipments for the first time — the combined backlog of ~$127B (+38% YoY) has no precedent in this data set, and no OEM has guided to capacity additions large enough to move the composite before late 2027.
▸ Critical-path risk has doubled in scope: Large-transformer lead times (92wk) remain the headline, but MV switchgear (42–55wk) is now co-constraining DC campus timelines — operators must pre-order two distinct equipment categories 18+ months ahead, compressing project scheduling flexibility to near zero for 2027 ready-for-service dates.
▸ Scarcity is monetizing — watch escalation clauses: OEM price increases of 8–12% on new transformer bookings and Eaton's 60–65% DC gross margins confirm the constraint is being harvested commercially; the key financial risk is now escalation clause exposure embedded in 2026–27 DC offtake agreements, not just delivery timeline slippage.

Monthly Commentary — March 2026
1
Backlog at structural highs across all six OEMs: Combined order book reached ~$127B (+38% YoY blended), with GE Vernova the most strained (+52% YoY). Schneider Electric (Smart Energy div.) and Siemens Smart Infrastructure both now included — each reporting B2B above 1.30. Order intake continues to outpace shipments for the 9th consecutive quarter — no relief visible in any near-term guidance.
2
Large transformer lead times breached 90 weeks: Domestic US ≥100MVA slots now quoting 92 weeks, up from 68wk a year ago and a 24-month avg of ~54wk. Import lead times (South Korea, India) tracking 58–70wk — still offering partial arbitrage for projects with 5-year+ timelines.
3
Switchgear moving into transformer territory: Medium-voltage switchgear (5–15kV class) quotes have slipped to 42–55 weeks, up from 28wk. Three major DC campus orders in Q1 cited switchgear as the critical-path item for 2027 RFSDs — a shift from transformers dominating the constraint narrative.
4
PDU lead times easing — only bright spot: 480V rack PDUs now quoting 22–28 weeks, down from a 34wk peak in Aug '25 as Eaton and Vertiv expanded capacity. Does not offset upstream bottlenecks but reduces interior fit-out risk for operators.
5
Price escalation accelerating into backlog: GE Vernova and Siemens Energy both disclosed price increases of 8–12% on new large transformer bookings in Q4 '25 earnings. Eaton flagged 60–65% gross margins on data-center-specific orders — constraint is monetizing. Watch for escalation clauses in 2026–27 DC offtake agreements.
