Why IQ Leads
Every datacenter must secure grid access through an ISO interconnection queue — a process that takes 2–5 years, making queue dynamics the earliest observable proxy for DC capex intentions. Filing a grid-connection request commits developer resources 2–4 years before a shovel touches the ground. Historically, queue reversals have preceded visible capex cuts by 12–18 months; given the unprecedented depth of the current backlog, that transmission lag could extend to 18–30 months. The 60% weight on net MW momentum is the dominant signal: when additions no longer exceed withdrawals, the committed pipeline is actively contracting. Withdrawal acceleration (20%) provides the earliest warning — outflow volumes tripled before net MW turned negative. Wait-time elongation (20%) captures the structural deterrent to new entrants that does not mean-revert without ISO reform.

Withdrawals edged down to 4,280 MW — second consecutive monthly decline, still near cycle highs: Feb '26 saw 4,440 MW, Mar '26 4,391 MW, Apr '26 4,280 MW. The deceleration improves the withdrawal acceleration sub-factor score, providing a modest composite lift alongside the net MW bounce. The underlying attrition level (4,280 MW/mo vs 1,700–2,000 MW in H1 2024) remains structurally elevated — this is normalization of pace, not a healing of the outflow rate.

Wait time ticked up to 4.4 years — P90 at 7.2 years signals growing dispersion in project outcomes: Average wait crossed 4.4yr for the first time. The rising avg-to-P90 gap (4.4yr vs 7.2yr) reflects selective attrition: straightforward projects proceed while capital-intensive projects requiring major transmission upgrades face indefinite delays. The largest projects — most consequential for DC capex — tend to withdraw first, making the queue look healthier on average than it is in terms of committed high-load capacity.

Capex transmission lag remains 18–30 months — reported numbers insulated through 2027: Even if the composite bottoms here, the pipeline feeding datacenter construction starts in 2028+ is already contracting. Hyperscaler projects that are past the queue stage (in GIA execution, equipment order, or construction) are insulated — GEV transformer backlog +52% YoY, 92-week lead times mean those builds will complete regardless. The queue signal is predictive of 2028–29 vintage capex, not the capex currently being reported.
