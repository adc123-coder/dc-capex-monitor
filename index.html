Sub-Factor Detail — March 2026
#1
Queue Reversal
…
…
Net MW turned decisively negative for the third straight month — March at −696 MW is the largest single-month outflow on record. The 3-month net of −1,001 MW is the first sustained negative sequence since tracking began, with additions at their 24-month low (4,862 MW) as the hyperscaler filing pipeline thins. Avg interconnection wait time has risen to 4.3yr — structurally mismatched with 18–24mo hyperscaler build schedules — accelerating queue-bypass strategies such as BTM generation and bilateral PPAs. FERC Order 2023 process improvements won't affect actual queue throughput before late 2027.
#2
DRAM Index
…
…
Price is near cycle high at $46.20, but momentum flipped negative for the first time since the Oct '25 squeeze began — the −4.7% MoM print ends a five-month streak of +34–65% monthly gains. Acceleration had already turned negative in February (the 2nd derivative leading indicator), and the March print deepens it further. The grey-market TF/BBG spread (10.4%) has narrowed from its 12.0% Jan '26 peak but remains well above the 4.5% calm-market baseline. High level + negative momentum + negative acceleration is the classic potential-top configuration: one more negative month would drag the composite toward zero and confirm the inflection.
#3
DC Permit & Starts
…
…
Deeply divergent signals — a speculative land and filing surge is masking deteriorating approval quality. Land acquisition ran at 4,812 acres in March (+31% MoM), and permit velocity hit a 24-month high at 247/mo, both driven by pre-positioning ahead of anticipated site-selection tightening. However, the approval rate fell to 61% — the weakest in the 24-month window — and regulatory friction reached a cycle peak at 9 active blocks (Dominion Energy hold Jan, PJM NJ/NYC interconnection freeze Mar, Chicago ComEd 345kV delay Nov '25). The composite sits just above zero: the filing surge is masking fundamentally deteriorating conditions on the regulatory and utility side.
#4
Electrical Equipment
…
…
18–24 month transformer lead times force developers to commit slots well before capex hits any filing. Order intake continues to outpace shipments for the 9th consecutive quarter — no relief visible in any near-term guidance. Combined backlog reached ~$127B (+38% YoY blended), with B2B above 1.30 across all six OEMs. Large transformer lead times breached 90 weeks (92wk domestic, 58–70wk import, up from a 24-month avg of ~54wk). Switchgear has now moved into transformer territory: MV quotes at 42–55wk, with three major DC campus orders in Q1 citing switchgear as the critical-path item for 2027 RFSDs. Price escalation is accelerating — GE Vernova and Siemens Energy disclosed 8–12% increases on new bookings; Eaton flagged 60–65% gross margins on DC-specific orders. The constraint is monetising with no sign of demand abating.
#5
AI Financing Stress
…
…
Near its 24-month floor — private capital is repricing AI infrastructure risk. CoreWeave OAS at 218bps (vs. 90bps baseline), AI HY/IG spread premium at 312bps, and private Series B+ velocity at 31 deals/mo are all substantially below neutral levels. This index was the earliest of the five to cross below the Apr '24 baseline (Apr '25) and has remained negative for 12 consecutive months. Persistent spread elevation combined with a collapse in round velocity signals that private capital is becoming increasingly selective on DC-intensive AI capex — a dynamic that typically precedes reported capex deceleration by 6–12 months.
